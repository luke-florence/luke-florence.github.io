summary: "SPUN exists to map and protect the mycorrhizal networks that underpin life on Earth — and they do it with a rare combination of scientific rigour, global reach, and genuine urgency. Their advocacy, media work, and commitment to supporting researchers across the global south have made them one of the most inspiring organisations in mycological science. It is hard to overstate how much their mission has shaped and energised my own research. SPUN funded the sequencing of arbuscular mycorrhizal fungi in my third PhD chapter, which investigates how soil nitrogen and drought interact to shape mycorrhizal fungal communities across the Eastern and Southeast Australian Temperate Forests — forest ecoregions that are global hotspots for mycorrhizal diversity and are almost entirely absent from the existing global dataset. Their support is making it possible to put Australia on the global mycorrhizal map."
