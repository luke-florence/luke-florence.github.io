summary: "My relationship with the Global Soil Mycobiome Consortium goes back to my undergraduate years, when Leho Tedersoo — one of the world's leading fungal ecologists and the driving force behind the GSMc — generously guided me through the fungal sequencing analysis that became my Tea Tree mycorrhizal project. That early mentorship left a lasting impression. Since then, I have contributed over 130 soil samples to the GSMc dataset, helping to put Australia on a global fungal map that had long underrepresented the Southern Hemisphere. The consortium's ambition — to characterise the soil mycobiome at planetary scale — is exactly the kind of bold, collaborative science I want to be part of. I look forward to continuing to contribute Australian samples and to working with the GSMc as we push towards comprehensive, continent-wide fungal mapping using the most advanced sequencing and analytical methods available."
