summary: |
  My relationship with the Global Soil Mycobiome consortium goes back to my undergraduate years, when Leho Tedersoo — one of the world leaders in fungal ecology and biogeography, and the driving force behind the GSMc — generously guided me through the fungal sequencing that contributed to my tea tree mycorrhizas project. That early mentorship left a lasting impression.

  Since then, I’ve contributed over 130 soil samples to the GSMc dataset, helping to put Australia on a global fungal map that has long underrepresented the Southern Hemisphere. The consortium’s ambition — to characterise the soil mycobiome at a global scale — is exactly the kind of bold, collaborative science I want to be part of.
  
  I look forward to continuing to contribute Australian samples and to working with the GSMc as we push toward comprehensive fungal mapping using the most advanced sequencing and analytical methods available.
