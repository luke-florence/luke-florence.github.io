summary: |
  The Australian Microbiome Initiative is exactly the kind of large‑scale, collaborative, open‑access infrastructure Australian science needs more of. Spanning thousands of sites across the continent, it has fundamentally expanded what is possible for microbial research in Australia.

  During my PhD, I was fortunate to draw on their soil fungal dataset to produce a ground‑up reanalysis that curated and benchmarked the fungal records for biodiversity and conservation research. Beyond the data, the initiative generously supplied soil and DNA samples to supplement my own field sites for my PhD chapter nitrogen and mycorrhizal fungi in Australian temperate forests.

  Their openness and collaborative spirit have been genuinely enabling, and I hope the work I’ve done with their data gives something back to the initiative and the community it serves.
