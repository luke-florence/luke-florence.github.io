summary: "The Australian Microbiome Initiative is exactly the kind of large-scale, collaborative, open-access infrastructure that Australian science needs more of. Spanning more than 2,200 georeferenced soil sites across the continent, it has fundamentally changed what is possible for microbial research in Australia. I was fortunate to leverage their dataset as the foundation for my second PhD chapter — AusMycobiome — a ground-up reanalysis that curated and corrected the fungal records to make them ready for biodiversity and conservation research. Beyond the data, the Australian Microbiome Initiative generously supplied me with soil and DNA samples to supplement my own fieldwork sites for my third PhD chapter. Their openness and collaborative spirit have been genuinely enabling, and I hope the work I have done with their data gives something back to the initiative and the community it serves."
