  The Australian Wildlife Society has championed the protection of Australia’s unique wildlife for decades, backing it with the kind of on‑the‑ground commitment that genuinely moves the needle for species and ecosystems that can’t speak for themselves. It is deeply motivating to receive support from an organisation that understands research is essential to real conservation outcomes.

  AWS funded fieldwork and laboratory consumables during my PhD, helping me get my hands dirty digging up dirt across temperate forests to sample the hidden fungal communities that sustain these ecosystems. I’m grateful for their support and for what they stand for — the idea that caring for wildlife isn’t a hobby, it’s an obligation.
