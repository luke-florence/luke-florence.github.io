summary: "The Australian Wildlife Society has championed the protection of Australia's unique wildlife for decades — not as a distant institutional concern, but with the kind of on-the-ground commitment that actually moves the needle for species and ecosystems that can't speak for themselves. There is something deeply motivating about receiving support from an organisation that genuinely believes research matters for conservation outcomes. AWS supported the fieldwork and laboratory consumables for my third PhD chapter, helping me get boots on the ground across the Eastern and Southeast Australian Temperate Forests to sample the hidden fungal communities that sustain these ecosystems. I am grateful for their support and for what they represent: the idea that caring about wildlife is not a hobby — it is an obligation."
